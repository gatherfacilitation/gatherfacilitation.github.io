Facilitation for personal healing journeys, conflict transformation and group processes to explore inner landscapes and creativity.

Approach

Why "Gather"? Gather is my favorite verb. I feel most fulfilled, most satisfied, most at ease when I have all of my people around me, when my community is robust and supportive. I always infuse my work with this sense of enough-ness, engaging our deepest human yearnings to be connected and to feel whole both within ourselves and with each other.

Offerings

Counseling

1:1 sessions to help individuals in their journeys towards deeper alignment with their life paths, purposes & dreams.

Conflict Transformation

Individual coaching and group processes to move from conflict and harm to mutual understanding and potential.

Group Facilitation

Social justice workshops, conflict resolution trainings, meeting facilitation, community building and curated creative experiences.

Past Events

Feeding the Self—interactive multi-sensory shabbat experience | November 2019

So often, we eat rushing, standing up, while talking to friends. The intimate relationship between ourselves and our food is diminished. What happens to our taste buds when our sight is blocked? What does it take to bring pleasure to the act of feeding ourselves? Participants will be asked to wear a blindfold and observe silence while they are served a 4-course meal.


Feeding Another—interactive multi-sensory shabbat experience | December 2019

What is our relationship to giving and receiving nourishment? How do we negotiate these themes in relationship? Participants will be asked to bring a partner to dinner, during which they will only be able to feed one another - never themselves - and communicate non-verbally.

“White | Woman | Shame | Blame | Game”: workshop | June 2019 (Co-facilitated with Annie-Rose London)

Moving past guilt in service of becoming stronger anti-racist humyns. This workshop explores the ways shame and guilt - particularly as internalized by female-identified and assigned people - intersect with white fragility and vigilance and can make us, as white women, less resilient and capable as anti-racists.


How does our guilt and shame and lack of self-assurance make us fragile and terrified of "messing up" with race, and thus afraid to speak out or over-react when being called out/in? How does it make us hyper vigilant of other white people, severing ties with them rather than leveraging our access and privilege to build bridges and educate? This workshop focuses on working out the experiences of white-identified women and AFAB folks of all genders.

I am an artist & facilitator extraordinaire who loves working at the intersections of creativity and connection. I am excited to help groups transform conflict and explore collective healing. I also love working with individuals, supporting them on the journey to align with their life purposes and dreams. I dance equally with the sacred & the profane - may we always make space for joy & play!

My studies and training in Process Work facilitation, Hakomi somatic psychotherapy, restorative practices, social justice and anti-oppression work - combined with my five years of experience in operations management - have equipped me with empathy, compassion and a keen understanding of the ways in which systems of oppression and inequitable distribution of power impact people at both interpersonal and institutional levels, as well as a complex understanding of relational dynamics.

My work is infused with a spiritual tenor, as I weave in learnings from my Jewish heritage and Tree of Life, my two-year women's rites of passage journey. I am never without a creative project, be it curating aesthetically-pleasing spaces, cooking, preserving, macrame-ing, screen-printing, watercoloring, woodworking, laser-cutting, face-painting, songwriting or planning a dinner party.
